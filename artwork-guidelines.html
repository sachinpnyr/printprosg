Artwork Guidelines

Follow these guidelines to ensure your print files produce the best possible results. Not sure? Our design team can prepare your file for you.

Get Design Help

Quick Checklist

File format: PDF (preferred), AI, EPS
Colour mode: CMYK
Resolution: minimum 300 DPI
Bleed: 3mm on all sides
Safe zone: 3mm inside trim edge
Fonts: embedded or outlined

File Format

Preferred: PDF with embedded fonts and CMYK colour mode.

Also accepted: AI (Adobe Illustrator), EPS (Encapsulated PostScript), high-resolution JPG or PNG (300 DPI minimum).

Not accepted: Word documents, PowerPoint files, or web-resolution images (72 DPI).

Colour Mode

All files must be in CMYK colour mode. RGB files will be converted to CMYK automatically, which may cause noticeable colour shifts — especially for vibrant reds, blues, and oranges.

For Pantone/spot colours, please specify the CMYK equivalent. We do not support spot colour printing on standard digital products.

Bleed & Safe Zone

Bleed: Add 3mm of extra artwork beyond the trim edge on all sides. Background colours and images that touch the edge must extend into the bleed area.

Safe Zone: Keep all important content (text, logos, key graphics) at least 3mm inside the trim edge to avoid being cut off.

Trim line: The actual finished size of your product.

Resolution

Minimum 300 DPI at actual print size for all standard products. Images that look sharp on screen at 72 DPI will appear blurry when printed.

For large format products (banners, foam board, signage), 150 DPI at actual print size is acceptable.

Fonts

All fonts must be embedded in the PDF or converted to outlines/curves in AI/EPS files. Missing fonts will be substituted with a default font, which may change your layout.

Minimum recommended font size: 6pt for body text, 8pt for important information.

Black & Dark Colours

For large solid black areas, use Rich Black: C:60 M:40 Y:40 K:100 for a deeper, richer black.

For small text (under 12pt), use Pure Black: C:0 M:0 Y:0 K:100 to avoid colour registration issues causing blurry text.
